Peanut Butter & Jelly — Pseudocode Algorithm

The goal of this activity was to write a precise step-by-step algorithm for making a PB&J sandwich — detailed enough that someone with no prior knowledge (or a very literal robot) could follow it perfectly. I tried to describe every micro-action: which end of the knife to hold, how to wipe it clean before switching from jelly to peanut butter, and how to align the two slices before pressing them together. This exercise taught me that ambiguity in an algorithm causes bugs, and that being overly specific is always better than assuming context.

  1. Open the bag of bread from the opening end
  2. Take out two pieces of bread from the bag and place them on the plate
  3. Set the bag aside
  4. Separate the two pieces of bread on the plate
  5. Take the jelly jar and twist the lid until it opens
  6. Set the lid aside
  7. Hold the non-sharp end of the knife
  8. Insert the sharp end of the knife into the jelly jar
  9. Gather a spoonful of jelly on the blade
  10. Move the knife over one slice of bread while keeping the jelly balanced
  11. Tilt the knife so the jelly drops onto the bread
  12. Spread the jelly evenly until 80% of the bread is covered
  13. Lay the knife down beside the plate
  14. Take the jelly jar lid
  15. Align the lid with the jar opening and twist until closed
  16. Take a tissue
  17. Hold the knife by the non-sharp end
  18. Wipe both sides of the blade clean with the tissue
  19. Set the knife and tissue aside
  20. Take the peanut butter jar and twist the lid until it opens
  21. Set the lid aside
  22. Hold the non-sharp end of the knife
  23. Insert the sharp end into the peanut butter jar
  24. Gather a spoonful of peanut butter on the blade
  25. Move the knife over the second slice of bread while keeping the peanut butter balanced
  26. Tilt the knife so the peanut butter drops onto the bread
  27. Spread the peanut butter evenly until 80% of the bread is covered
  28. Lay the knife down beside the plate
  29. Take the peanut butter jar lid
  30. Align the lid with the jar opening and twist until closed
  31. Pick up the peanut-butter slice
  32. Flip it and place it spread-side down on top of the jelly slice

05 — Implementing Algorithms

This activity focused on translating algorithm logic into actual Scratch code — boolean conditions, loops, and variables. I kept my logic clean by giving each question its own set of variables rather than reusing names. The skills I practised here (conditional branching, loop control) directly helped me when I built the Simon Says project later in the unit. Simon Says — Flowchart

Simon Says was the culminating project for Unit 1. I designed the full game logic as a flowchart before coding it, which taught me the value of planning before writing a single line of code. The flowchart maps every possible state: waiting for input, checking if the sequence is correct, advancing to the next round, and triggering the game-over condition.
